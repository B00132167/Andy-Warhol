Factory Pop-Art

Like many artists Warhol required a sanctuary where he could work on his masterpieces. For Andy Warhol, this was his studio in New York known as ‘The Factory.’ Although ‘The Factory’ was where Warhol worked on his art it was also known for its wild parties attended by celebrities. Over the years ‘The Factory’ was housed in numerous locations across New York Throught Warhol's life. However the name was always the same

‘The Factory’ was not only used for Warhol's paintings but also for his films. Andy Warhol made a great deal of films in the various locations for 'The Factory' These include films such as 'The Velvet Underground and Nico: A Symphony of Sound' from 1966 and 'Bad' from 1977.
